The E & S

Employee & Self-employed

An employee work for someone and don't have risk of running a business. But they need to feel secure in their work and need to see it in writing. They need to reduce their fear for 'secure' by the presence of some degree of certainty. So they seek security and strong agreements when it comes to employement.
The idea of security is more important than money. Employees can be presidents of companies or janitors. It's not so much what they do, but the contractual agreement they have with the person or organization that hires them.
Self employers run their own work. They don't like to depend on other people for their income. If they work hard, they expect to get paid for their work well. They also have understood that if they don't work hard, then they don't desire to be paid much. So they have fiercely independent souls.

Business owner is almost oppositeof self employed. They like to surround themselves with smart people. Unlike self employed who doesn't like to delegate work, business owners like to delegate.
Investors make money with money. They don't have to work becausr their mone is working for them. Investor quadrant is the playground of the rich. If people want to be rich someday, they ultimately must come to that quadrant. In there money is converted to wealth.

The B

Business owner

This group of people could almost be the opposite of the S. Those who are true B’s like to surround themselves with smart people from all four categories: E, S, B, and I. Unlike the S, who doesn’t like to delegate work (because no one can do it better), the B likes to delegate. The true motto of a B is, “Why do it yourself when you can hire someone to do it for you, and they can do it better?”
Henry Ford fit this mold. As one popular story goes, a group of so-called intellectuals came by to condemn Ford for being ignorant. They claimed he really didn’t know much. So Ford invited them into his office and challenged them to ask him any question, and he would answer it. So this panel assembled around America’s most powerful industrialist and began to ask him questions. Ford listened to their questions and, when they were through, he simply reached for several phones on his desk and called in some of his bright assistants and asked them to give the panel the answers they sought.
One of the quotes credited to Ford goes: “Thinking is the hardest work there is. That is why so few people engage in it.”

The I

Investor

Investors make money with money. They don’t have to work because their money is working for them.
I quadrant is the playground of the rich. Regardless of which quadrant people make their money in, if they hope someday to be rich, they ultimately must come to the I quadrant. It’s in the I quadrant that money becomes converted to wealth.
